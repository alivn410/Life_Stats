Earning Power – income sources / career growth One basic income source, low growth. No clear plan to increase earnings. Stable main income. Financial Literacy – understanding money / taxes / investing Knows basics only (salary in, spending out). Little understanding of tax, interest, or investing. Saving – discipline to store value Saves randomly, if anything is left at the end of the month. Has a fixed % auto-saved monthly (e.g. 10–20%). Builds emergency fund slowly. Strong saving habit. Investing – growing money smartly Keeps money mostly as cash. Afraid of investing or does random risky moves. Invests a portion into simple, diversified options (e.g. index funds) and understands risk. Has a clear, long-term investing strategy linked to goals (retirement, property, etc.). Start small and long-term, avoid gambling behaviour. Money working for you while you sleep. —
Spending Habits – impulse control / value spending Impulse buys, emotional spending, no tracking. Tracks spending roughly. Most purchases are intentional, with some slips. Spends mostly on high-value things (health, growth, experiences); very low waste. Wait 24 hours before big non-urgent purchases. Spending aligned with your values and long-term goals. —
Risk Management – not over- or under-taking risks Either too scared (misses all opportunities) or too reckless (big random risks). Takes small/medium calculated risks. Has basic protections (emergency fund, low debt). Balanced risk profile. Uses insurance, diversification and good planning. Decide risk level before acting: “How much can I afford to lose here?” Calm, controlled relationship with risk. —
